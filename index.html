No 1    PUMPKIN SOUP

 

1 kilo pumpkin

1 onion

2 rashers bacon (optional)

1 litre chicken stock (1-2 stock cubes)

salt and pepper to taste 


1.    Remove rind from bacon and roughly chop.

2.    Peel and roughly chop onion.

3.    Peel and roughly chop pumpkin.

4.    Heat saucepan pan and add bacon to saucepan and heat stirring constantly for 2 minutes. (No fat is needed as bacon provides fat)

5.    Add onions and cook till they are soft but not brown.

6.    Add pumpkin, stir well, cover and allow to “sweat” for 5 minutes on a low heat.

7.    Add stock and cook covered for 20-25 minutes till vegetables are cooked.

8.    Puree soup until smooth.

9.    Reheat season to taste and serve. 



No 2    LEEK AND POTATO SOUP

 

3 leeks

1 onion

2 large potatoes

2 rashers bacon (optional)

1 litre chicken stock (1- 2 stock cubes)

salt and pepper to taste

2 extra potatoes

 

1.    Trim the top and bottom of the leeks, remove outer leaves, wash well.

2.    Slice leeks thinly.

3.    Peel and roughly chop onion.

4.    Peel and slice potatoes.

5.    Remove rind from bacon and roughly chop.

6.    Heat saucepan pan and add bacon to saucepan and heat stirring constantly for 2 minutes. (No fat is needed as bacon provides fat)

7.    Add onion, leeks and potatoes stir well, cover and allow to “sweat” for 5 minutes on a low heat.

8.    Add stock and cook covered for 15-20 minutes till vegetables are soft.

9.    Puree soup until it is smooth.

10.    Peel and dice extra potatoes into 2cm cubes. Place in a saucepan of boiling water and cook till soft, approx. 10 minutes. Drain well.

11.    Return the soup to the heat add the drained potato cubes, season to taste and heat to serve.



No 3    MINESTRONE SOUP


1 onion

1-2 rashers of bacon

1 cup green beans (frozen)

1 large carrot

2 sticks celery

1 large potato

¼ cabbage

½ cup broken pasta pieces

2 tablespoons rice

1 litre stock (1-2 beef stock cubes)

1x425gm tomatoes

1-2 tablespoons tomato paste

125gm red kidney beans (optional)

salt and pepper to taste

½ cup grated parmesan cheese to garnish


1.    Prepare vegetables;

Peel and dice onion.

Peel and dice carrot.

Wash and chop celery.

Peel and dice potato.

Wash and shred cabbage.

Chop beans into smaller pieces.

2.    Trim rind from bacon and chop finely.

3.    Dry fry bacon and onion. (No oil needed as fat with bacon)

4.    Add stock, carrot, celery and potato.

5.    Puree tinned tomato and add.

6.    Bring to the boil and cook till vegetables are almost cooked, 15-20 minutes.

7.    Add beans, cabbage, rice, pasta and tomato paste, stir well. Simmer for 12-15 minutes stirring occasionally.

8.    Add kidney beans and season to taste, heat through and serve garnished with parmesan cheese.



No 4    CHICKEN AND LEEK SOUP, FOR MICHELLE

 

2 chicken thigh fillets

2-3 leeks

1 litre water

1-2 bay leaves

salt and pepper to taste

1 chicken stock cube (optional) 


1.    Place chicken, bay leaves, salt and pepper in a large saucepan and cover with 1 litre of cold water

2.    Bring slowly to the boil and simmer gentle for 30 minutes.

3.    Strain stock and save, but discard bay leaf.

4.    Trim the top and bottom of the leeks, remove outer leaves, wash well and slice thinly.

5.    Place leeks in saucepan and cover and allow to “sweat” for 5 minutes over a low heat.

6.    Finely shred the cooked chicken and add to leeks with the saved stock.

7.    Return to heat and cook for 10 minutes till leeks are cooked.

8.    Season to taste with salt and pepper and stock cube, if desired. Serve

No 6    BACON AND VEGETABLE SOUP, FOR MICHELLE

 

Piece bacon bone

1 litre water

pepper

2 rashers bacon

1x425gm tomatoes

1 large carrot

2 sticks celery

½ green capsicum

½ red capsicum

1 small zucchini

1 onion

 

1.    Place bacon bone and pepper in a large saucepan and cover with a litre of cold water.

2.    Bring slowly to the boil and simmer gently for 1 hour.

3.    Strain stock and save, discard bacon bone.

4.    Trim rind from bacon and dice finely.

5.    Dry fry bacon in saucepan for 3-4 minutes.

6.    Peel and finely dice carrot and onion.

7.    Wash and finely dice celery, red and green capsicum and zucchini.

8.    Place all vegetables in saucepan with bacon and stir well.

9.    Cover and allow to “sweat” for 5 minutes over a low heat.

10. Remove fat from the saved stock.

11. Puree tomatoes and add with saved stock. 

12. Cook for 20 minutes till vegetables are cooked. Season with salt and pepper. Serve. 



No 7    NANNA’S VEGETABLE SOUP

 

Soup bones

½ cup soup mix

2 litres water

2-3 large carrots

1 large onion

4 sticks celery

1 turnip

1 swede

1-2 parsnips

salt and pepper

 

1.    Place soup bones, soup mix, roughly chopped onion, salt and pepper in a large saucepan and cover with 2 litres of cold water.

2.    Bring slowly to the boil and simmer gently for 1-1 ½ hours.

3.    Removes bones from stock. Cool stock allowing fat to set and then remove.

4.    Peel and roughly cut carrots, turnip, swede and parsnips.

5.    Wash and roughly chop celery.

6.    Add all vegetables to stock and bring to the boil.

7.    Simmer till vegetables are cooked, approx. 30 minutes.

8.    Cool slightly before pureeing. Heat and season to taste. Serve.



No 8    RON'S PEA AND HAM SOUP

 

2 Cups green split peas

750 gm ham bones

2 ½ L water

1 stick celery

1 carrot 

1 onion

3 leeks

1 potato

 

Method

1.    Soak split peas in cold water for several hours or overnight.

2.    Wash or peel vegetables and chop roughly.

3.    Place bones, drained peas, water, celery, carrot and onion in a large pan.

4.    Bring to the boil and simmer, covered for 2 hours, or until peas are tender.

5.    Add leeks and potato, cook for another 30 minutes, or till vegetables are tender.

6.    Remove the ham bones from the pan, chop the meat and set aside, discard bones.

7.    Cool the soup mixture slightly. Puree with a blender or push through a sieve.

8.    Return to pan and add chopped meat and heat through.

9.    Serve



No 9    SPICED CARROT SOUP WITH COCONUT CREAM

 

1 Tb olive oil

1 onion, finely chopped

2 cloves garlic, crushed

1 small red chilli, seeds removed and finely chopped

1 tsp grated fresh ginger

500gm carrots, peeled and chopped

1 medium sweet potato, peeled and chopped

2 kaffir lime leaves

4 cups chicken stock

juice ½ -1 lime

1 Tb sugar

200 ml coconut cream

 

Method

1. Heat oil in pan over medium heat, add onion and cook for 2-3 minutes or until softened.

2. Add garlic, ginger, chilli, carrot, sweet potato, lime leaves and stock.

3. Bring to the boil reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes.

4. Allow to cool slightly, then puree in a blender.

5. If you want it finer pass through a sieve, then season.

6. Return to pan with lime juice, and sugar and gently reheat.

7. Add coconut cream heat and serve.

 



No 10    ROASTED CARROT AND HERB DUMPLING SOUP

 

1 kg carrots, peeled and roughly chopped

340g brown onions, roughly chopped 

2 Tb olive oil

salt and pepper

1 tsp ground cumin

6 cups vegetable stock

1 cup cooking cream


Herb Dumplings

1 cup SR flour

2 Tb chopped parsley

2 Tb chopped chives

salt and pepper

½ cup milk

 


1.    Preheat oven 180.

2.    Place the carrots, onions, oil, salt, pepper and cumin in a baking dish and toss to coat.

3.    Roast for 30 minutes or until the carrots are tender and golden. (This is a good time to make dumplings)

4.    Place vegetables in a food processor, add half of the stock and blend until smooth.

5.    Place in a large saucepan and stir in the remaining stock.

6.    Add the cream and bring to the boil over medium heat.

7.    Add the dumplings, cover with a tight fitting lid, and reduce heat.

8.    Cook for 6-8 minutes, or until dumplings are cooked through.


To Make Dumplings

1.    Place the flour, chives, parsley, salt and pepper in a bowl and make a well.

2.    Add the milk and stir until just combined.

3.    Place the dough on a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth. 

4.    Divide the dough into eight and roll into balls. Cover and set aside until needed.



No 11    SPICED PUMPKIN SOUP

 

2 Tb Olive oil

1 onion, finely chopped

1 leek, white part only, finely sliced

1 clove garlic, crushed

1 teaspoon ground cumin

½ teaspoon ground coriander

½ teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg

1 kg peeled pumpkin, diced

1 large potato, peeled, diced 

1 L chicken stock or vegetable stock

½ cup thin cream

 


1.    Heat oil in a large saucepan over low heat, add onion and leek. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until softened but not coloured.

2.    Add garlic, cumin, coriander and nutmeg and cook for 30 seconds to develop the flavour.

3.    Add pumpkin, potato and stock and bring to the boil. Turn heat low, cover and simmer for 30 minutes.

4.    Allow to cool then puree.

5.    Stir through cream, reheat gently. Season, add a little more nutmeg if desired. Serve.



No 12    SWEET POTATO & CORIANDER SOUP

 

2 Sweet potato, diced

2 sticks celery, sliced

2 carrots, diced

1 onion, chopped

2 teaspoons garlic, crushed

1 L chicken stock 

165 gm coconut milk

2 Tb chopped coriander

Coriander to garnish

 


1.    Heat oil in a large saucepan over low heat, add onion and cook for 2-3 minutes, until softened but not coloured.

2.    Add garlic, sweet potato, celery, carrots and coriander. Cover and allow to “sweat” for 5 minutes over low heat.

3.    Add stock and bring to the boil. Turn heat low, cover and simmer for 30 minutes.

4.    Allow to cool then puree.

5.    Stir through coconut milk, and reheat gently. Season and garnish with coriander

No 7    SPINACH TRIANGLES

 

1pkt Filo pastry

½ bunch spinach

125gm ricotta cheese

250gm grated matured cheese 

2 spring onions

pinch nutmeg

spray oil

 

1.    Trim spring onions and chop finely.

2.    Mix together ricotta, matured cheese, spring onion and nutmeg.

3.    Open filo pastry and lay out flat and cover with Glad wrap to prevent it drying out.

4.    Take one sheet and spray lightly with spray oil.

5.    Cover with another sheet of filo and spray and top with a third sheet.

6.    Cut into 7 even strips across the width.

7.    Place a small teaspoon of the cheese mixture at the base of each strip.

8.    Fold pastry diagonally over filling. Continue folding up, across, up across etc to the top forming a triangle.

9.    Place on a lightly greased over tray and spray with oil.

10.    Bake at 180C for 12-15 minutes until golden brown.

11.    Cool and store in an airtight container. They may soften slightly but will crisp up when reheated.

A    ROASTING


Roasting uses tender cuts of meat cooked in the oven by dry convected heat.


Preheat oven to 180C.

Remove most visible fat.

Place in baking dish fat side up. You may choose to use a rack so meat is not sitting in any fat.

Bake 30 minutes for each 500gm of weight.


For rare meat cook at a higher temperature 200C for less time.

Higher temperatures cause more shrinkage, therefore less meat.


If you feel meat may be tough, often cheaper cuts of meat can be, add a cup of water to pan after first 30 minutes.


Add vegetables for roasting 1 hour before meat will have completed cooking.


Remove meat from oven and stand 10-15 minutes before cutting. This will make it easier to cut and helps retain juices in the meat keeping it tender

 

Make gravy at this time. Drain any fat from pan. Add water to pan and heat, when boiling thicken with flour and or gravy powder and flavour as desired.


Meats suitable to roast:


Lamb        Loin, leg, shoulder.

        Traditional served with mint sauce.


Beef        Sirloin, rump, round, rolled roast, blade, fillet.


Pork         Leg, loin, shoulder, fillet.

        Traditional served with apple sauce.

C    GRILLING


Grilling is a quick method of cooking small tender pieces of food by dry radiant heat.


Always preheat grill.

Trim fat from meat.

Always turn meat with tongs.

Turn meat only when one side is cooked to keep the juices in the meat and the meat tender. Over turning dries out meat.

Only salt meat if desire when meat is cooked as salt dries and toughens meat.


Cooking time depends on the thickness of the meat and the preference of the diner.

No 53    POTATO GNOCCHI

COOKING TERMS


Blanch:    To place food in cold water and bring to the boil. This is done to remove strong flavours and to whiten. It is how you remove the skin from an almond, when recipe asks for blanched almonds.


Canape:    Small shapes of fried bread, toast, pastry or dried biscuits on which savouries are served.


Cream:    When butter and sugar are beaten together, with beaters or a  wooden spoon till sugar is dissolved and the butter a lighter colour. The mixture then looks like cream and is very light in texture.


Dice:        Small cubes of food.


Dry fry:    Foods cooked in a non stick pan without any fat added as they are foods that are already fatty, eg bacon, mince.


Garnish:        A decoration for food.


Glaze:    To brush with a liquid, eg milk before cooking scones or Oetker glaze to coat and give a shine to fruit in a tart.

Oetker glaze can be purchased at continental shops and some super markets.


Hors D’oeuvres:    An appetizer course of a meal.


Knead:    To work a flour mixture on a board by hand until a smooth dough is formed.


Marinade:    A mixture of oil and acid in which food is soaked to developed flavour and tenderness.


Michelle:    Recipes referring to Michelle will contain very little to no carbohydrates. This means there will be NO SUGAR, and very little flour. Also excludes bread, rice, pasta, potatoes and pumpkin.


Puree:    Food which has been cooked and mashed through a sieve or processed in a food processor till smooth and all lumps removed. 


Rub in:    Using the finger tips to work butter into flour till it looks like fine breadcrumbs. The finger tips are used as they are the coldest part of the hand.


Season:        Add salt and pepper to the taste you desire.


Simmer:    To bring a liquid to a temperature just below boiling point, and maintain that temperature for the period of cooking.


Syrup:        A solution of sugar and water.


Whip:        To beat rapidly to incorporate air and increase volume.
